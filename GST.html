GST LAWS & INDIRECT TAXATION

1. FOUNDATION OF GST

1.1 Introduction & Core Principles

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax system levied on the supply of goods and services. It was introduced in India on July 1, 2017, via the 101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016.

  • Destination-Based Consumption Tax: Tax revenue accrues to the jurisdiction where the final consumption takes place rather than where goods or services originate.
  • Value Addition Model: Tax is collected at every stage of the supply chain, allowing tax paid on procurement as an offset via Input Tax Credit (ITC).
  • Removal of Cascading Effect: By allowing seamless credit flow, GST eliminates the "tax-on-tax" structure of earlier indirect tax regimes.

1.2 Subsumed Taxes & Exclusions

Jurisdiction Level Subsumed Taxes
Central Level Central Excise Duty, Service Tax, Additional Customs Duty (CVD), Special Additional Duty (SAD), Central Cesses and Surcharges.
State Level State VAT/Sales Tax, Central Sales Tax (CST), Entry Tax/Octroi, Entertainment Tax, Luxury Tax, Taxes on Lotteries.
Key Constitutional Exclusions
Alcoholic liquor for human consumption remains constitutionally outside GST. Petroleum crude, high-speed diesel, motor spirit (petrol), natural gas, and aviation turbine fuel are constitutionally included but temporarily kept outside GST until notified by the GST Council.

1.3 Dual GST Model

India adopted a Concurrent Dual GST Model to uphold national federal architecture, allowing both the Union and the States/Union Territories to simultaneously tax supply transactions.

GST Structure
Intra-State Supply
(Within same State/UT)
CGST (Centre) + SGST / UTGST (State/UT)
Inter-State Supply
(Between two States/UTs)
IGST (Collected by Centre, apportioned to States)
  • Central GST (CGST): Levied by the Central Government on intra-state supplies.
  • State GST (SGST) / UTGST: Levied by State Governments or UTs (with or without legislature) on intra-state supplies.
  • Integrated GST (IGST): Levied by the Centre on inter-state supplies and imports. IGST is collected by the Centre and subsequently apportioned between the Centre and the destination State.

1.4 GST Council (Article 279A)

The GST Council is a constitutional body established under Article 279A of the Indian Constitution to serve as the apex decision-making body for indirect tax policy.

  • Composition: Union Finance Minister (Chairperson), Union Minister of State in charge of Revenue/Finance, and Finance/Taxation Ministers of each State Government.
  • Quorum & Decision Threshold: Quorum requires 50% of total members. Decisions require a 3/4th (75%) majority of weighted votes of members present and voting.
    • Central Government Vote Weight: 1/3rd (33.33%) of total votes cast.
    • State Governments Vote Weight: 2/3rds (66.67%) of total votes cast collectively.

2. CGST ACT, 2017 — IMPORTANT SECTION 2 DEFINITIONS

All definitions under Section 2 are structured categorically for regulatory clarity.

2.1 Supply & Transaction Definitions

Section 2(1) — Actionable Claim: Claim to any unsecured debt or beneficial interest in movable property not in possession. Treated as goods; however, under Schedule III, only specified actionable claims (lottery, betting, gambling, horse racing, online gaming) are taxable.
Exam Trap
All actionable claims are NOT taxable; only specific ones (e.g., lottery, betting, gambling, online gaming) are subject to GST. Claiming insurance money is a non-taxable actionable claim.
Section 2(31) — Consideration: Any payment made or to be made, or any act or forbearance, in respect of, in response to, or for the inducement of supply (monetary or non-monetary). Includes payments by recipient or third parties, but excludes subsidies provided by Central or State Governments.
Section 2(47) — Exempt Supply: Supply attracting a nil rate of tax or wholly exempt under Section 11 (CGST) or Section 6 (IGST), including non-taxable supply.
Exam Trap
Exempt supply includes non-taxable supply. Do not confuse Exempt Supply with Zero-Rated Supply (exports are zero-rated, not exempt).
Section 2(52) — Goods: Every kind of movable property other than money and securities. Includes actionable claims, growing crops, grass, and things attached to or forming part of the land agreed to be severed before supply.
Section 2(78) — Non-Taxable Supply: Supply of goods or services not leviable to tax under CGST or IGST Act (e.g., alcoholic liquor for human consumption, petroleum products).
Section 2(102) — Services: Anything other than goods, money, and securities. Includes activities relating to the use of money or its conversion for which a separate consideration is charged.
Section 2(108) — Taxable Supply: A supply of goods or services or both which is leviable to tax under the CGST Act.
Section 2(119) — Works Contract: A contract for building, construction, fabrication, erection, installation, fitting out, repair, maintenance, renovation, or alteration of any immovable property wherein transfer of property in goods is involved.

2.2 Stakeholders, Persons & Location Definitions

Section 2(5) — Agent: A person (including factor, broker, commission agent, del credere agent, or auctioneer) who carries on the business of supply or receipt of goods/services on behalf of another.
Section 2(17) — Business: Includes trade, commerce, manufacture, profession, vocation, or any activity for consideration, regardless of volume, frequency, or pecuniary benefit (profit motive is NOT necessary).
Section 2(20) — Casual Taxable Person (CTP): A person who occasionally undertakes transactions involving supply in a State/UT where he has no fixed place of business.
Section 2(50) — Fixed Establishment: A place (other than registered place of business) characterized by a sufficient degree of permanence and suitable structure (human and technical resources) to supply or receive services.
Section 2(56) — India: The territory of India, including territorial waters, seabed and subsoil, continental shelf, exclusive economic zone (EEZ), and airspace above India.
Section 2(68) — Job Work: Any treatment or process undertaken by a person on goods belonging to another registered person (ownership remains with the principal).
Section 2(69) — Local Authority: Municipality, Panchayat, Municipal Committee, Zila Parishad, or any authority legally entitled to manage local funds.
Section 2(71) — Location of Supplier: Place of business or fixed establishment from where supply is made, or place most directly concerned with the supply.
Section 2(77) — Non-Resident Taxable Person (NRTP): Any person who occasionally undertakes supply of goods/services but has no fixed place of business or residence in India.
Section 2(84) — Person: Includes Individual, HUF, Company, Firm, LLP, AOP/BOI, Corporation, Local Authority, Government, Trust, and Artificial Juridical Person.
Section 2(86) — Place of Supply: Location where supply is deemed to occur, determined as per Chapter III of the IGST Act.
Section 2(90) — Principal Place of Business: Place of business specified as the principal place in the certificate of registration.
Section 2(93) — Recipient: Person liable to pay consideration. Where no consideration is payable, the person to whom goods are delivered/made available or service is rendered.
Section 2(105) — Supplier: Any person supplying goods or services or both, including an agent acting on behalf of such supplier.
Section 2(107) — Taxable Person: A person who is registered or liable to be registered under Section 22 or Section 24.

2.3 Turnover, Tax & Credit Provisions

Section 2(6) — Aggregate Turnover: Total value of all taxable supplies (excluding inward supplies under RCM), exempt supplies, exports, and inter-State supplies of persons having the same PAN, calculated on an all-India basis. Excludes CGST, SGST, UTGST, IGST, and Cess.
Section 2(11) — Assessment: Determination of tax liability under GST, including self-assessment (Sec 59), provisional assessment (Sec 60), scrutiny assessment (Sec 61), and best judgment assessment (Sec 62).
Section 2(19) — Capital Goods: Goods whose value is capitalized in the books of account of the person claiming ITC and used in the course or furtherance of business.
Section 2(59) & 2(60) — Input & Input Service: Any goods (other than capital goods) or services used or intended to be used by a supplier in the course or furtherance of business.
Section 2(61) — Input Service Distributor (ISD): Office of the supplier receiving tax invoices for input services and issuing a prescribed document to distribute credit to branches having the same PAN.
Section 2(62) — Input Tax: Tax charged on supply of goods/services including RCM tax paid and IGST on imports; excludes tax paid under Composition Scheme.
Section 2(63) — Input Tax Credit (ITC): Credit of input tax available to offset output tax liability.
Section 2(82) — Output Tax: Tax chargeable on taxable supply made by a taxable person or agent; excludes tax payable under reverse charge.
Section 2(98) — Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM): Liability to pay tax by the recipient of supply instead of the supplier.

3. SCOPE OF SUPPLY, LEVY & CHARGEABLE EVENTS

3.1 Scope of Supply (Section 7)

Supply is the ultimate taxable event under GST.

  • Section 7(1)(a) — Supply for Consideration: Includes sale, transfer, barter, exchange, license, rental, lease, or disposal made for consideration in the course or furtherance of business.
  • Section 7(1)(b) — Import of Services: Import of services for consideration, whether or not in the course or furtherance of business.
  • Section 7(1)(c) — Schedule I Transactions: Activities treated as supply even without consideration:
    1. Permanent transfer/disposal of business assets where ITC has been availed.
    2. Supply between related or distinct persons in the course of business (including branch transfers).
    3. Supply between principal and agent.
    4. Import of services by a person from a related person or establishment outside India for business.
  • Section 7(1)(d) — Schedule II Classification: Classifies whether transactions constitute a supply of Goods OR Services (does NOT determine supply existence).
    • Supply of Goods: Title transfer in goods; transfer of business assets.
    • Supply of Services: Renting immovable property, works contracts, software licensing, temporary IP transfer.
  • Section 7(2) — Schedule III (Non-Supplies): Activities outside GST purview:
    • Services by employee to employer in course of employment.
    • Services by courts/tribunals, MPs, MLAs, and constitutional authorities.
    • Sale of land and sale of completed building (after completion certificate).
    • Funeral, burial, crematorium, or mortuary services.

3.2 Levy & Collection of CGST/IGST (Section 9 & Section 11)

  • Section 9(1): Charging section for CGST on intra-state supplies at rates not exceeding 20%.
  • Section 9(3) — Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM): Recipient pays tax on notified goods/services (e.g., Legal services by advocates, GTA, Sponsorship).
  • Section 9(4) — RCM on Unregistered Suppliers: Applicable only to specified classes of registered persons/goods.
  • Section 11 — Exemptions: Absolute exemption (mandatory; no tax payable) vs. Conditional exemption (optional based on conditions satisfied).

3.3 Composition Levy (Section 10)

Simplified scheme for small taxpayers (aggregate turnover up to ₹1.5 Crore; ₹75 Lakh for special category States) paying tax at a fixed percentage of turnover without ITC benefit.

4.2 Value of Supply (Section 15)

Value of supply equals the Transaction Value (price actually paid/payable when parties are unrelated and price is sole consideration).

  • Mandatory Inclusions [Sec 15(2)]: Non-GST taxes/duties, incidental expenses (packing, loading), third-party payments borne by recipient, late fees/interest for delayed payment, and price-linked non-government subsidies.
  • Exclusions [Sec 15(3)]: Pre-supply discounts shown on invoice, or post-supply discounts established by agreement linked to specific invoices where recipient reverses proportionate ITC.

5. INPUT TAX CREDIT (ITC) PROVISIONS

5.1 Eligibility & Core Conditions (Section 16)

  • Must possess tax invoice, debit note, or prescribed tax document.
  • Must have received goods or services.
  • Tax charged must have been actually paid to the Government.
  • Must have filed valid returns under Section 39 (GSTR-3B).
  • 180-Day Payment Rule: Recipient must pay supplier within 180 days from invoice date; failure leads to ITC reversal with interest.

5.2 Apportionment & Blocked Credit (Section 17)

Section 17(1) & (2): Proportionate credit allowed exclusively for business and taxable supplies (Rules 42 & 43 apply). Banks can opt for 50% flat monthly ITC under Sec 17(4).

Section 17(5) — Blocked Credits (No ITC)
ITC is explicitly disallowed on: Motor vehicles (with specific passenger exceptions), food & beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services, club memberships, works contract services for immovable property construction (capital account), personal consumption, and goods lost, stolen, or destroyed.

5.3 Special ITC Cases & Job Work (Sections 18, 19, 20)

  • Section 18: Special eligibility upon new registration, voluntary registration, or switching from composition to regular scheme (5% reduction per quarter for capital goods).
  • Section 19 (Job Work): Principal can claim ITC on inputs/capital goods sent to a job worker. Inputs must return within 1 year; capital goods within 3 years (else deemed supply).
  • Section 20 (ISD): Input Service Distributor allocates input service credit across branches based on turnover.

6. REGISTRATION, INVOICING, RETURNS & PAYMENTS

6.1 Registration Provisions (Sections 22, 23, 24, 25, 29)

  • Section 22 Thresholds: Normal States: ₹40 Lakh (Goods) / ₹20 Lakh (Services). Special Category States: ₹20 Lakh (Goods) / ₹10 Lakh (Services).
  • Section 23 Exemption: Persons dealing exclusively in exempt supplies or agriculturists.
  • Section 24 Compulsory Registration: Inter-state taxable suppliers, CTPs, NRTPs, RCM recipients, E-commerce operators, and ISDs must register irrespective of turnover threshold.
  • Section 25 Procedure: Application within 30 days of liability on PAN-basis. Establishments in different States are treated as Distinct Persons.
  • Section 29 Cancellation: Proper officer can cancel for non-filing of returns (6 continuous months for regular; 3 quarters for composition).

6.2 Invoicing & Documents (Sections 31, 33, 34, 35)

  • Section 31 Time Limit: Goods: Before or at removal/delivery. Services: Within 30 days (45 days for banks) from supply.
  • Section 34 Notes: Credit Notes (reduce tax liability for returns/defects) & Debit Notes (increase tax liability).
  • Section 35/36 Accounts: Books of accounts must be retained for 72 months (6 years) from the due date of filing the Annual Return.

6.5 Interest, TDS, TCS & Refunds

  • Section 50 Interest: Delay in tax payment: 18% p.a. (on net cash liability). Wrong ITC availed & utilized: 24% p.a.
  • Section 51 TDS: 2% tax deduction by Government entities on contracts exceeding ₹2.5 Lakh.
  • Section 52 TCS: Up to 1% tax collection at source by e-commerce operators on net value.
  • Section 54 Refund: Application within 2 years from relevant date. Exporters get 90% provisional refund within 7 days. Subject to unjust enrichment unless proven.

7. AUDIT, ENFORCEMENT, PENALTIES & APPEALS

7.1 Audit & Enforcement Powers (Sections 65, 66, 67)

  • Section 65 (Departmental Audit): Conducted by officers at business premises; 15 days prior notice; complete within 3 months.
  • Section 66 (Special Audit): Directed by Deputy/Assistant Commissioner; conducted by external CA/CMA; complete within 90 days.
  • Section 67 (Inspection, Search & Seizure): Triggered by Joint Commissioner authorization on "reason to believe" tax evasion occurred.

8. INTEGRATED GST (IGST) ACT & CUSTOMS ACT PROVISIONS

8.1 IGST Act Key Sections

  • Section 5 (Levy of IGST): Charged on inter-state supplies and imports (IGST = CGST + SGST).
  • Section 7 (Inter-State Supply): Location of Supplier ≠ Place of Supply; includes imports, exports, and SEZ units.
  • Section 8 (Intra-State Supply): Location of Supplier = Place of Supply (excludes SEZ and foreign trade).
  • Section 10 & 11 (POS Goods): Location where movement terminates; for imports, location of importer.
  • Section 12 & 13 (POS Services): General rule: Location of recipient. Exceptions apply for immovable property, physical performance, passenger transport, and intermediary services.
  • Section 16 (Zero-Rated Supply): Exports & SEZ supplies. Option to supply under LUT without tax or pay IGST and claim refund.

8.2 Customs Act, 1962 Core Provisions

  • Section 12 (Charging Section): Customs duty levied on imports and exports.
  • Section 14 (Valuation): Assessable Value = Transaction Value on CIF basis (Cost, Insurance, Freight). Excludes post-importation expenses.
  • Section 15 & 16 (Relevant Dates): Imports: Date of Bill of Entry or Entry Inwards (whichever is later). Exports: Date proper officer passes order permitting clearance (Shipping Bill date).
  • Section 22 & 23 (Abatement/Remission): Section 22 grants duty abatement for damage; Section 23 remits duty for lost, destroyed, or abandoned goods.
  • Section 28 (Recovery): Demand notice within 2 years (normal) or 5 years (fraud/collusion).
  • Section 74 & 75 (Duty Drawback): Sec 74 grants 98% drawback on re-export of imported goods within 2 years. Sec 75 grants drawback on materials used in exported manufactured goods.
